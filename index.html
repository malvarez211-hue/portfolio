Who I Am

About Me

My name is Maria C. Alvarez and I am a bilingual (English/Spanish) Master of Social Work candidate at California State University, Dominguez Hills, graduating in May 2026. I was born and raised in the Los Angeles area, and my roots in this community are at the heart of everything I do as a social worker.

My practice is grounded in a deep commitment to equity, social justice, and culturally responsive care. I have worked with diverse populations across community health, mental health, and aging services settings — always centering the voices, strengths, and lived experiences of the people I serve.

I am passionate about addressing the systemic barriers that prevent marginalized communities from accessing the resources and care they deserve. Whether in direct practice or macro-level advocacy, I bring a Critical Race Theory lens, trauma-informed approach, and genuine dedication to my work.

Key Learning Experiences

The following three assignments demonstrate proficiency across all nine CSWE social work competencies, completed during the Master of Social Work program at California State University, Dominguez Hills.

KLE 1 MSW 531 — Research Methods ⭐ 25/25
Participatory Action Research Proposal
Competencies: 1, 3, 4, 6, 9

Reflective Narrative: I chose this assignment because it demonstrates my ability to use research to understand community issues and support social change. This project helped me strengthen my skills in research-informed practice and ethical decision-making. Designing a CPAR proposal around illegal trash dumping in South Central LA pushed me to think beyond the individual and consider how systemic racism and environmental inequity intersect. It also challenged me to center community voices as co-researchers rather than subjects, which deepened my understanding of participatory justice and my own role as an advocate.

Why I included this: This assignment showcases my ability to integrate CRT, environmental justice frameworks, and community-based research methods — skills central to macro social work practice.
KLE 2 MSW 582 — Mental Health Practice ⭐ 92/100
Recovery Narrative Paper Part 2
Competencies: 1, 2, 5, 9

Reflective Narrative: I chose this assignment because it demonstrates my understanding of how trauma and social support influence recovery. It also reflects my ability to analyze research and consider the cultural and systemic factors that affect mental health. Writing this paper challenged me to examine recovery not as a linear process but as something shaped by identity, community, and access to resources. It reinforced my commitment to providing culturally responsive, trauma-informed care — and reminded me why representation and lived experience matter in clinical practice.

Why I included this: This paper highlights my ability to connect research, policy, and cultural competency to real clinical practice — demonstrating depth in both micro and macro perspectives.
KLE 3 MSW 500 — Human Development ⭐ 37/40
Development Theory Paper
Competencies: 4, 6, 7, 8

Reflective Narrative: I chose this assignment because it demonstrates my understanding of human development theories and how they guide social work assessment and intervention with clients. Engaging with multiple theoretical frameworks — from Erikson to ecological systems theory — helped me see how development is never just biological but deeply shaped by environment, culture, and social context. This paper strengthened my ability to meet clients where they are developmentally and to tailor my approach based on the unique life stage challenges each person faces.

Why I included this: This assignment demonstrates my theoretical foundation in human development and my ability to apply multiple frameworks to assessment and intervention — core competencies for direct practice.
